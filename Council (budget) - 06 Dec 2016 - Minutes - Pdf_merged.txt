YMCA Funding
(Councillor LeSueur)

Moved by: Councillor LeSueur

WHEREAS Most municipalities support a swimming pool and recreational
programming - less than a third in the province do not have any recreation
programs; and

WHEREAS there is a minimum of 18 City/YMCA partnerships in the province
= of similar size communities: Quinte West (Trenton), Owen Sound,
Goderich/Port Colborne Strategic partnerships intended to meet the needs of
the community; and

WHEREAS the YMCA does not have an endless source of funds ~ this year
the YMCA is struggling to meet its annual campaign goal ~ greater
competition in the marketplace - new projects - all worthy causes to support
= we are currently $40,000 behind on our annual campaign goal ~ pockets
are only so deep; and

WHEREAS the YMCA will not turn anyone away - There is a gap between
what the YMCA raises and the financial support the YMCA provides; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is very concerned for the health of those living In our
communities; 59.9% of those people living in our communities are
overweight or obese - the loneliness factor is marked particularly among our
seniors; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is not a “CLUB” - it is a community gathering place -
helping individuals to reach their goals whether they be physical, mental or
spiritual - the YMCA works with the whole person; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is a values based charity supporting this community
since 1860- well governed by local people who work hard to ensure the
YMCA is financially viable, vibrant, well maintained and offers quality
programming; and

WHEREAS the YMCA Foundation is a separate entity to the YMCA operation ~
it has restrictions on more than half of the money contained within it; and

WHEREAS the YMCA Foundation Is a strategic entity designed to ensure the
long term viability of the YMCA ~ intended to meet community need over the
long term ; and

WHEREAS the YMCA Foundation is in its infancy and is just readying itself to
Invite investment; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is in need ~ Capital costs slated for the 2017 fiscal are
$300,000; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is an economic driver ~ 100 staff - trusted child care
provider enabling parents to work; and

WHEREAS 75% of those using Y programs live in Brockville proper, the rest
are from surrounding areas but when they come to Brockville to go to the
YMCA, they also shop and do business in Brockville; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is working hard to develop strategic partnerships to
better serve the community; and

WHEREAS the City of Brockville should be one of its strategic partners; and

WHEREAS the YMCA is eager to help the city reach its goals - ready and able
to partner to meet collective concerns;

NOW THEREFORE Council approves an on-going annual grant in the amount
of $25,000 to support the YMCA with unrestricted contributions for their
capital requirements.

CARRIED, AS AMENDED ON A LATER VOTE

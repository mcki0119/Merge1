Mayor's Remarks

Mayor Wren offered regrets on behalf of Councillor Dery, noted Councillor
Lavergne had joined the meeting remotely and that Councillor Earle is expected to
join the meeting late. He also noted a number of senior staff present and
welcomed them all to the meeting along with Art Koolwine, citizen member of the
Planning and Development Committee.

His Worship congratulated Fire Chief Melanie Jones for her recent appointment to
the position of Director with the Ontario Association of Fire Chiefs, noting his
expectation that she will make amazing contributions to the board.

Mayor Wren announced the annual staff holiday luncheon was held last week and
more than 150 staff attended. He said it was a great gathering of people who keep
our city moving. He specifically acknowledged the long-service of many staff and
the retirements of Lesley White, Gary Moore and Patti Lewi:

Mayor Wren announced that the Rotary Outdoor Rink opened yesterday and said a
daily schedule will be posted on-site and on the City's website with designated

2024-204
Proposed Amendments to Zoning By-law 050-2014
803 Chelsea Street

THAT Zoning By-law 050-2014 be amended to rezone lands described as
Part of Lot 14, Parts 1 and 2, Concession 2, County of Leeds, City of
Brockville, municipally known as 803 Chelsea Street, from "C4 - Highway
Commercial Zone", subject to "Source Water Protection” considerations to
"C4 - Highway Commercial Special Exemption Zone", subject to "Source
Water Protection" considerations to allow Business & Professional Offices
and Clinics or Medical Offices as permitted uses.

THAT the necessary by-law shall be enacted.

2024-208
Cash-In-Lieu of Parking By-law Follow-up Report

THAT a Cash-in-Lieu of Parking fee in the amount of $6,500.00 per space
be established, along with associated indexing of $300.00 per year for 10
years for approved reductions in parking of 3 or more spaces; and

THAT a Cash-in-Lieu of Parking fee in the amount of $2,500.00 per space
be established for 10 years for approved reductions in parking to a
maximum of 2 spaces; and

THAT a Cash-in-Lieu of Parking fee in the amount of $0.00 per space be
established for properties that are enrolled in an affordable housing
program funded municipally, provincially or federally and receive
reductions in parking to a maximum of 2 spaces; and

THAT the necessary by-law be approved.

New Business - Report from Members of Council

Affordable Housing Proposal
Councillor Earle

Moved by: Councillor Earle
THAT Council waive building permit fees for affordable housing; and
THAT Council waive development charge fees for affordable housing; and
THAT affordable housing be defined as:

a.2-bedroom one, bathroom, under $300,000.00,
b.3-bedroom two, bathroom, under $400,000.00,

THAT this would be for all affordable units, profit and non-profit.
REFERRED AS AMENDED ON A LATER VOTE

Motion to Amend
Moved by: Councillor Earle

THAT the motion be amended to substitute the word ‘affordable’ with
‘economical’ throughout.

For: Mayor Wren, Councillor Earle, Councillor Fullarton, Councillor Hobbs,
Councillor Kalivas, Councillor Lavergne, Councillor Severson, and
Councillor Wales
CARRIED 8-0 on a recorded vote

‘Motion to Refer
Moved by: Councillor Kalivas

THAT this matter be referred to staff with a request to provide information
regarding the concept of supporting affordable housing development and
to report back to the Planning and Development Committee meeting in
February with a range of paths forward.

For: Mayor Wren, Councillor Earle, Councillor Fullarton, Councillor

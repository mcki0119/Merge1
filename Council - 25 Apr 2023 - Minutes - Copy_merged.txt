LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT STATEMENT
MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Wren thanked everyone who was involved in the Brockville Community-
Wide Clean-up last weekend. A record-breaking, 1200 volunteers signed up and
almost 2000kg of litter were collected. His Worship thanked those on the Clean-up
Committee, comprised of Lyndsay d'Entremont, Councillor Cameron Wales, Max
Larocque, Mary Lynn Villeneuve, and Stephanie Dunbar; sponsors for the event,
which included La Knapp Construction, CN Rail and Home Depot} and City Staff
who cleaned up the parkette behind City Hall. Additionally, city staff continue to
clean up after the winter, sweeping streets and sidewalks, weeding garden beds;
they were thanked for their hard work in making the City look ready for summer,

His Worship thanked residents for their cooperation during the detours and road
closures due to the Halleck's Road bridge demolition; the Operations team,
Brockville Police and OPP were recognized for their great management.
Congratulations were passed along to the Brockville Music Festival Committee for
hosting their first event post-COVID. The festival opened on Sunday and will
conclude next Tuesday evening at the Brockville Arts Centre. Tickets are available
at the Box office for those who would like to attend.

Additionally, Mayor Wren announced that the United Way of Leeds and Grenville
are hosting Community Conversations. The Brockville event is being held
‘Thursday, April 27, 2023 from 6:30 to 8:30 pm at the Brockville Public Library.

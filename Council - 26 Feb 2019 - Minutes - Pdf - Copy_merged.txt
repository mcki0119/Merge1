PUBLIC MEETING UNDER THE BUILDING CODE ACT

‘The Mayor announced that Council is conducting a Public Meeting under the
Building Code Act with respect to proposed building permit fees for 2019. Council
intends to pass a by-law to impose fees under the Building Code Act. This Public
Meeting is for persons to make representation with respect to the proposed
building fees.

‘The proposed building permit fees schedule revisions would increase a number of
fees charged in the Building Permit application process.

Mayor Baker inquired if there were any persons present who wish to make oral or
written submissions on the proposed fees. No persons came forward.

The Public Meeting concluded at 7:05 pm.
MAYOR'S REMARKS.

‘The Mayor commended the Emergency Services and First Responders as well a
member of the public who intervened last week during an incident on the ice on
the river. Thanks to their efforts a bad situation was diffused. Mayor Baker was
happy to see another successful 30 Hour Palliative Care Telethon. He praised this,
great group of volunteers and our generous community raising just under
$225,000. In addition, he applauded the first class staff and volunteers at the
Brockville Art Centre. The Mayor stated that this past weekend the Aquatarium
held another Farmer's Market that saw approximately 3,000 people attend. Also,
the Ontario Senior Championships were just held at the Brockville Country Club,
another facility in the City to be proud of. Mayor Baker explained that coming up
on March 5 from 12 pm to 5 pm there will be an Active Transportation "Pop up”
Information session at the Brockville Public Library. Everyone is welcome to
come and discuss their ideas and/or concerns regarding active transportation in
the City

DELEGATIONS

1. Heritage Merit Award
(P. Bullock, Chair of Heritage Brockville)

Paul Bullock explained that Doug Grant has recently decided to retire from
Heritage Brockville after a number of years. He then offered a brief review
of many of the things Doug Grant has been involved in over the years. He
discussed that Doug had been a big part of the creation and design of
much of the historical signage in downtown Brockville. Paul Bullock noted
that Doug had been one of the founding member of what was called
LACAC, an acronym for Local Architectural Conservation Advisory
Committees. On behalf of Council Mayor Baker offered their appreciation of
the work Doug has done over the years.

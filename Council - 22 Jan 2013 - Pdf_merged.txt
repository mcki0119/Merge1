WHEREAS Canadian municipalities have expressed growing concerns with trade
agreements and their potential impacts on municipal programs and services and local
autonomy; and

WHEREAS unfettered access to Canadian municipal procurement by European
corporations may encourage privatization and reduce economic development options for
local communities; and

WHEREAS this proposed legislation has become the contre of much debate at both the
Association of Municipalities of Ontario and the Federation of Canadian Municipalties, in
‘both of which the Corporation ofthe City of Brockville maintains membership: and

WHEREAS this trade deal could lead to implications on our abilty to operate as an
‘effective governing body: and

WHEREAS the provincial and territorial governments have been actively involved in
negotiating CETA with the European Union

‘THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the Corporation ofthe City of Brockville resolve
that:

+ City Council request the Province of Ontario issue a clear, permanent exemption of the
ity of Brockville from the Canada-European Union Comprenensive Economic and
‘Trade Agreement (CETA) and that it otherwise protect the powers of municipalties,
hospitals, schoo! boards, ullites, universities and other sub-federal agencies to use

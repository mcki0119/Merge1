Mayor's Remarks

Mayor Wren offered regrets from Councillors Deery and Earle and noted Councillor
Lavergne may be late this evening. He acknowledged the number of attendees in
the gallery, many of whom were present as representatives of the Rotary Clubs of
Brockville and thanked them for being in attendance.

His Worship recognized award recipients and nominees of the annual Brockville
and District Chamber of Commerce Awards Gala held recently, noting he was
unable to attend but provided a certificate of congratulations to each recipient on
behalf of Council. He made special mention of the Brockville Arts Centre as the
recipient of the Tourism Award and commented on the wonderful work and
dedication of staff.

Mayor Wren announced that September 25th is Franco Ontarian Day and said
there will be a corresponding event held beginning at 11:00 am at Tunnel Bay
where he and Councillor Lavergne will provide remarks in both official languages.

Mayor Wren noted September 23-29 is Rail Safety Week, the goal of which is to
reinforce the importance of being safe and taking great care near rail
infrastructure that transects the city.

September is also Big Brothers and Big Sisters (BBBS) awareness month and
Mayor Wren highlighted the incredible work of BBBS of Leeds Grenville staff,
supporters and volunteers. His Worship noted there are more than 300

Mayor Wren announced Monday, September 30th as the National Day for Truth
and Reconciliation and said a principal gathering is planned to take place at Hardy
Park from 10:00 am to 4:00 pm. He reminded all to take time to reflect on the
legacy of the residential school system and the 94 calls to action resulting from
the Commission on Truth and Reconciliation.

Regarding the Pumping Station replacement at Centeen Park, His Worship
announced a Public Information Session scheduled for Thursday, October 3 from
4:00 to 7:00 pm and invited those interested to drop in during the time slot and
provide Input into the design of the station.

Finally, Mayor Wren acknowledged the passing of former Fire Chief Harold Tulk
and offered condolences to his family and friends. He said Mr. Tulk began his long
career in the fire service in 1970 and rose to the rank of Chief in the City of
Brockville and subsequently at the City of Kingston where he retired from. His
Worship said the flags at Tunnel Bay will be flying at half-mast in honour of former
Chief Tulk.

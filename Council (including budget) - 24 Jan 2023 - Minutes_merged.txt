LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT STATEMENT
MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Wren thanked all department heads for being present as they work through
the budget. He expressed condolences, on behalf of Councillors and the City of
Brockville, to retired Chief of Police, Ray McFadden, on the passing of his wife, as
well as Fire Chief, David Lazenby, on the passing of his mother.

His Worship reminded the community that upcoming changes to the waste and
recycling collection, moving to a 4-day cycle, will come into effect the week of
February 6th. The updated collection map and more information is available on the
City website, has been shared to social media pages and was featured in
newspaper advertisements. The next regular Council meeting on February 14th,
will start at 5:30 pm opposed to 6:00 pm. Mayor Wren noted that Councillor Wales
will arrive late to the meeting as he returns from the ROMA conference.

1. 2023-5
Ernie Fox Quay - 2022 Season electrical rebate

THAT Council approve a rebate in the amount equivalent of $4.00 per foot
to 2022 seasonal boaters of the Ernie Fox Quay harbour an

THAT the rebate exclude seasonal boaters at the Ernie Fox Quay harbour
who terminated their lease or have been issued part or full refunds and;

THAT the rebate be funded by the surplus in harbour electricity billing that

resulted from no hydro being available during the 2022 boating season.
CARRIED

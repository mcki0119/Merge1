MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Baker read a thank you card from the residents of Morristown expressing
the enjoyment they received from the River of Lights Festival held at Blockhouse
Island. He noted the figure skating competition that was being held in Brockville
on January 17th and 18th. He noted that hockey tournaments are often touted as
positive impacts for the restaurants and hotels, but he wanted to highlight this
competition as well.

His Worship spoke of the Official Opening of the P and G Pavilion at the Rotary
Park held on January 9th. Proctor and Gamble made a significant financial
contribution for the Pavilion. The other partner for this development is the Winter
Classic Committee. The Committee has been a partner through the development
of the outdoor space and will continue in the development of the space.

Mayor Baker and Councillor Wales announced the implementation of the Brockville
Transit System on Google Maps.

1. 2019-189-12
Active Transportation Plan Review

THAT the Active Transportation Plan be considered when resurfacing or
rebuilding a street that is part of the proposed network; and

‘THAT the City prioritize short-term focus for Active Transportation projects
on the Brock Trail and Active Transportation facilities directly connected to
the Brock Trail, including meeting both AODA standards and the Brock Trail
standard of 3m wide asphalt trails with 5% maximum slopes; and

‘THAT that all Active Transportation pavement markings be installed and
maintained by Public Works staff as per Ontario Traffic Manual Book 18;
and

‘THAT staff consider the Active Transportation Plan when reviewing and
recommending Traffic Calming options; and

‘THAT any future on-road cycling facilities or bike lanes be maintained as
seasonal bike lanes as per the existing bike lane on King Street West; and

THAT staff review and report back to Council with recommendations for
By-law changes including adding 6m No Parking areas in front of
crosswalks and Brock Trail entrances; and

THAT staff review and report back to Council regarding upgrading existing
Courtesy Crosswalks to Pedestrian Crossovers as recommended in the
Draft Active Transportation Plan; and

‘THAT that the Lower Speed Limits discussion in the Draft Active
‘Transportation Plan be considered by staff as part of the upcoming Traffic
Calming reporting; and

‘THAT the City currently focus Active Transportation project priorities on
infrastructure rather than programming as recommended in the Draft
Active Transportation Plan; and

THAT in-boulevard multi-use trails be proposed in future when developing
Active Transportation segments where possible, rather than on-road
facilities; and

THAT additional line painting be completed to delineate the parking lanes
from the driving lanes on Laurier Boulevard and Windsor Drive; and

THAT Planning staff report back with any recommended changes to the
Official Plan to include aspects of the Draft Active Transportation Plan.

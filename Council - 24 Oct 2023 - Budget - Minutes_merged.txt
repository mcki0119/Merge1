LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT STATEMENT
MAYOR'S REMARKS.

On behalf of Council, his Worship expressed condolences to the family of Neil
McHugh, a retired city employee who passed away recently. The mayor
explained that Neil had worked for the former Brockville PUC and transitioned to
City Operations. He noted that Nell was best known as the voice of the
Brockville Braves.

Mayor Wren also passed along sympathies to retired Finance Department staff
member Alexis Skeete on the passing of her husband, Brian.

Mayor Wren noted that last week he had attended the 4th Annual Awards of
Excellence Gala held by the Brockville & District Chamber of Commerce and
wished to congratulate all the recipients.

His Worship announced that the Remembrance Day recognition begins tomorrow
‘morning, when he pins the first poppy on Korean War Veteran and Branch 96
Legion Member, Ray Gilroy. Mayor Wren encouraged everyone to take a walk
downtown to view the Remembrance banners that are gracing the streets. He
also reminded everyone of the Remembrance lawn signs available through the

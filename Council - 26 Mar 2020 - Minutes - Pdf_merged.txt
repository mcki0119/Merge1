SUSPEND RULES OF PROCEDURE
1. Walve Notice Provision of Council's Procedural By-law
2020 03 26 Council Audio

Moved by: Councillor Bursey

WHEREAS on March 17, 2020 the Province of Ontario declaring an
emergency under s 7.0.1 (1) the Emergency Management and Civil
Protection Act; and

WHEREAS the Corporation of the City of Brockville is taking proactive
measures to limit the spread of the virus and to protect our residents,
businesses, visitors and staff which includes social distancing and
encouraging that people stay at home; and

WHEREAS Council desires to ensure the continued operation of the
municipality;

NOW THEREFORE Council waives the provisions in its By-law No. 113-
1980, being a By-law to Govern the Proceedings of Council, to summon a
Special Meeting of Council without 48 hours notice to consider the business
as listed on this agenda.

Fullarton, Councillor Journal, Councillor Kalivas, Councillor
Lavergne, Councillor Wales, and Councillor Wren
CARRIED 9-0 on a recorded vote

MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Baker thanked everyone for their cooperation during this time. There is
currently one reported case of COVID-19 by the Lanark, Leeds and Grenville
Health Unit. The City leadership team anticipated and planned for this. Be
assumed that our water and wastewater systems are operating safely, and
precautions are being taken to ensure continued operations. His Worship
expressed the importance of social distancing and the City is doing its part by
reducing non-essential services and taking steps to protect staff during this time.
Reduced City services will continue for the foreseeable future including the closure
of city facilities including playgrounds, library and the Aquatarium. The transit
system is operating with reduced service, but services will be expanded within the
next week while ensuring the safety of staff and riders. Brockville has not declared
an emergency at this time.

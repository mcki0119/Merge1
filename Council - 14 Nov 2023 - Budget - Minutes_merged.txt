MAYOR'S REMARKS.

On behalf of Council, His Worship extended their sincere sympathies to Councillor
Mike Kalivas and the Kalivas Family on the recent passing of Mike's mother, Mrs.
Jeanne Kalivas. Mrs. Kalivas was well known to many in the community as co-
owner of the business she co-founded with her late husband, Jon’s Restaurant, in
1960.

Mayor Wren shared that since the last meeting, the father of former Mayor David
Henderson passed away, Mr. Lyle Henderson. The mayor was able to see former
Mayor Henderson in person and pass along condolences to the Henderson family
on behalf of Council.

‘The mayor thanked Branch 96 of the Royal Canadian Legion for once again
planning and carrying out the City’s annual Remembrance Day Ceremony, along
with involvement of the Brockville Rifles, our Air Cadets and first responders.

Mayor Wren reminded residents that this Is the final curbside leaf collection week,
on your regular collection day leaves in biodegradable bags will be picked up.

‘The annual Tim Horton's holiday Smile Cookie campaign is happening this week
and are thankful to the Joyce family for choosing Loaves and Fishes as the charity
to receive the proceeds of that campaign this year. Mayor Wren explained that
you can participate at all the local Tim Horton's stores.

‘The very popular City led festival, the River of Lights kicks off next weekend in
sync with the annual Rotary Santa Claus Parade. The City is grateful to the many
sponsors and supporters of this initiative, along with all of the city staff who are
working hard to put it all together. You can enjoy the River of Lights nightly from
Saturday, November 25 until January 2nd.

His Worship described that the city is providing information to the community
about a major upcoming infrastructure project that will take place at the edge of
Centeen Park. The information session will be held here in Council Chambers on
November 30th from 4-7 p.m. The project is replacing the current sewage
pumping station with a new facility and the proposal is to build the new station
next to the current one, and then remove the current structure. More information
is on the city website.

LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT STATEMENT

MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Wren noted that Councillors Deery, Severson and Kalivas have sent their
regrets this evening.

His Worship offered congratulations to Downtown Brockville for a wonderful
event on Saturday, July 15th. It was a very busy day downtown and it was
great to see the community and visitors supporting our downtown businesses,
and all businesses in fact. The next street event is August 19th.

Mayor Wren mentioned the Twilight Makers Market at the railway tunnel which is
a monthly event. The next one will be August 15th from 4-7 pm with an array of
foods, crafters and artisans.

The Thousand Islands Poker Run will be taking place a week from this Saturday,
on August 1Sth, with a stop in Brockville's harbour at 12 noon. His Worship
noted that the harbour will be closed from 10 am to 3 pm that day. In addition,
he noted that it's that time of year again for the ever-popular Ribfest in support
of Big Brothers Big Sisters. It will be held at Hardy Park on the weekend of
August 10th to the 13th.

Mayor Wren welcomed many members of the Brockville Fire Department who
are in attendance this evening as Council will be officially appointing the new Fire
Chief, Melanie Jones, to her role. His Worship noted that he was very pleased to
have the new Chief here in Chambers this evening so that he can officially
welcome her and introduce her to Council and the Community. Mayor Wren
discussed the new Chief's past career and educational accomplishments.

Mayor Wren took the opportunity to thank the Fire Service for their role in the
significant fire event that occurred just two weeks ago. He wanted to thank
them again for their professionalism, skill and bravery, and the Deputy Chief,
Chris Paul, who managed the situation with incredible leadership.

Chief Jones thanked the Mayor for the warm welcome and stated she Is looking
forward to working with this great team of firefighters.

2022-60
Ontario Land Tribunal

WHEREAS Ontario municipalities, in accordance with legislation and as
approved by the province, rely on Official Plans that meet current
Provincial Planning Policy to ensure that planning and development meet
the needs of our community; and

WHEREAS it is within Council's authority to approve or deny Official Plan or
Zoning By-law amendments; and

WHEREAS municipal planning decisions may be appealed to the Ontario
Land Tribunal (OLT; formerly the Ontario Municipal Board or “OMB”, who
has the authority to make a final decision on planning matters based on a

City of Brockville
Council Meeting Minutes - Tuesday, April 12, 2022

Page 6 of 11

“best planning outcome” and not whether the proposed development is in
compliance with the municipalities Official Plan; and

WHEREAS towns and cities across this Province are repeatedly forced to
spend millions of dollars defending Official Plans that have already been
approved by the province in expensive and time consuming OLT hearings
which can add years to the development approval process and act as a
barrier to the development of attainable housing; and

NOW THEREFORE BE IT HEREBY RESOLVED that the Council for the
Corporation of the City of Brockville requests the Government of Ontario
be requested to immediately engage municipalities to determine an
alternative land use planning appeals process in order to dissolve the OLT".

FURTHER that a copy of this Motion be sent to the Honourable Doug Ford,
Premier of Ontario, the Minister of Municipal Affairs and Housing, the
Leader of the Opposition, the Leaders of the Liberal and Green Party, all
MPPs in the Province of Ontario; the Large Urban Mayors’ Caucus of
Ontario, the Small Urban GTHA Mayors and Regional Chairs of Ontario and
to the Association of Municipalities of Ontario (AMO) for their
consideration.

CLOSED MEETING MATTERS
LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT STATEMENT
MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Wren reminded everyone to slow down and drive with care as children head
back to school next week. He also shared his appreciation for the hard wark of the
summer students who were employed for the city.

‘The Mayor advised that Councillor Deery was unable to attend in person this
evening but will be joining remotely shortly.

His Worship noted that the roads that have been milled will start to be paved early
next week.

Mayor Wren offered an update on the scene of the Perth Street fire. On July 10th
the Chief Building Official issued a clean up order, the order has expired without
the work being completed, the owners were notified, and the City will step in.
These costs will be added to the property's taxes. He explained that a Safety
Inspection was carried out, a contract has been signed and they will be begin the
clean up in the upcoming days.

Mayor Wren spoke on the topic of the Musi-theatre. He reiterated that once
members of Council have voted on and made decisions, it becomes law, any one
member who chooses to go against what has been voted on is egregious. His
Worship said Councillor Fullarton has agreed to put forward a motion of
Reconsideration which, if passed by Council, will bring the item back to the
General Committee meeting on September 19th at which time there will be
opportunity for delegations.

DISCLOSURE OF INTEREST
Councillor Wales declared a Conflict of Interest with regards to a possible Motion
for Reconsideration regarding the Lease Renewal for the Musi-theatre at the
Brockville Art Centre.

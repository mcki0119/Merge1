lem 7.1 ~ Asphalt/Goncrete Program

Councillor Deery asked if the lower cost of oil would have a bearing on the
‘amount budgeted for asphalt. Mr. Cosgrove responded that the lower oil costs
‘should result in some savings. He further explained that the amount budgeted
averages replacement of the City's 312 lane kilometers on a 25 year life cycle.
With arterial roads requiring replacement at between 15 and 20 years and local
road between 40 and 50 years, the amount meets what should be budgeted
annually. In 2014, approximately 12 lane kms were replaced. When tenders,
result in savings, the savings are typically used to replace additional lane kms.

Moved by: Councillor Baker

‘THAT the entire funding amount of $700,000 be spent on asphalt/concrete
projects including any surplus over the budgeted 12 lane kms costs,

CARRIED

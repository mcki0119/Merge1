MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Baker reported the River of Lights event is averaging over 3,000 cars visits
per day. Thank you to all the people who have organized and are working at this
event. His Worship stated that the St. Lawrence Corridor Economic Development
Commission continue to partner with our neighbouring municipalities along the
river to bring additional jobs to the region. A video was shown to overview the
work of the Commission in 2020. He highlighted the commercial and industrial
business collaborations that have taken place because of the Industrial Corridor
Group meetings.

His Worship noted that the closing of the property transaction to purchase the
former Woolworths building has been delayed but assured residents that it should
be completed shortly.

Mayor Baker noted that he participated in a meeting with the Ontario
municipalities, Premier Ford and other Ministers discussing the distribution of
COVID vaccines. He reminded residents to continue with COVID protocols.

1. _ Draft Visitor Wayfinding Sign Policy
Moved by: Councillor Fullarton
THAT report SR2020-174 Draft Visitor Wayfinding Sign Policy be received.
CARRIED as amended on a later vote
Motion to Amend
Moved by: Councillor Fullarton
THAT the policy be amended to:

* delete the reference to referring sign requests to the Tourism
Committee to determine whether the sign Is eligible; and

* add the clause that at staff discretion additional directional signs can
be added where the distance between wayfinding signs is significant;
and

* to add Rotary Park the splash pad, pump track, outdoor rink and ball
diamonds to parks that will have visitor signage; and

+ to add ball parks where tournaments take place to List A; and

THAT the policy, as amended be adopted.

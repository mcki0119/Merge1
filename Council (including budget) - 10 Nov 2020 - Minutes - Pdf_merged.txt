MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Baker highlighted the development that has been taking place in Brockville
this year, including three industrial expansions, Canarm, Northern Cables and 3Ms
ew mask plant. A new residential neighbourhood has been approved near the
Youth Arena. The former Recorder & Times building on California Avenue has been
purchased by a company that will be building modular homes. The Hampton Inn is
hear completion. A new plaza is under development on Stewart Blvd at Parkedale
Avenue.

Brockville City Council has issued a challenge to the residents of Brockville to eat
three meals over three days, November 24th to 26th, from local restaurants. The
One Cold Night Sleep Out event for homeless awareness is taking place on Friday
November 13th at the tunnel plaza. The CPHC has partnered with 1000 Islands
Toyota to undertake a drive to provide stockings to isolated seniors this season
The collection takes place this Friday and Saturday nights.

His Worship announced that the Rotary ice pad is scheduled to open December
Ist, weather dependent. He also announced that skating will be brought back to
Tunnel Bay this season. Preparations are underway at Laurier Hill for tobogganing,
skating and ball hockey in parking lot.

Mayor Baker noted that the 2019 audit is nearing completion and is projecting a
surplus upwards of $1,000,000. The audit reports will be coming forward to
council within the next month.

Mayor Baker announced that the City has purchased the former Woolworth's
building on King Street West from the Regional Group with a closing date of
December 1st. The plan is to engage developers to refurbish and repurpose the
property. Further information will be before council in December.

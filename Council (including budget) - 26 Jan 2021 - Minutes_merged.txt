MAYOR'S REMARKS.

Mayor Baker announced that the outdoor skating locations of Laurier Hill and
Tunnel Bay are open which will operate for the community to use safely.

His Worship noted that board and committee meetings have been suspended since
the beginning of the COVID except for the Arena Advisory Committee who have
been tasked to complete their report to Council. He reported that recent COVID
numbers remain steady. It is hoped that this will result in current restrictions
being loosened in our area.

Mayor Baker requested feedback from local businesses and the community seeking
their opinions on the creation of a fund for local businesses that have been
impacted by the pandemic. More information to follow.

CORRESPONDENCE, COMMUNICATIONS AND PETITIONS

For:

Black History Month
(Councillor Wren)

Moved by: Councillor Wren

THAT Council hereby proclaim the month of February 2021 as Black
History Month in Brockville, in order to recognize and celebrate the many
achievements of Black Canadians who, throughout history, have done so
much to make Canada the culturally diverse, compassionate and
prosperous nation it is today.

Mayor Baker, Councillor Wren, Councillor Bursey, Councillor Earle,
Councillor Fullarton, Councillor Journal, Councillor Kalivas,
Councillor Lavergne, and Councillor Wales

CARRIED 9-0 on a recorded vote

National Flag of Canada Day
(Councillor Wren)

Moved by: Councillor Wren

THAT Council hereby proclaim Monday, February 15 as National Flag of
Canada Day in Brockville, in celebration of the 56th Birthday of our flag
and in recognition of Brockville’s place in the story of its creation,

For: Mayor Baker, Councillor Wren, Councillor Bursey, Councillor Earle,
Councillor Fullarton, Councillor Journal, Councillor Kalivas,
Councillor Lavergne, and Councillor Wales
